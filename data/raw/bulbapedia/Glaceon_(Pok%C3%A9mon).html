Glaceon (Japanese: グレイシア Glacia) is an Ice-type Pokémon introduced in Generation IV.

It evolves from Eevee when leveled up near an Ice Rock or when exposed to an Ice Stone.

(Specifics may differ in past games. Refer to Game data→Evolution data for these details.)

It is one of Eevee's final forms, the others being Vaporeon, Jolteon, Flareon, Espeon, Umbreon, Leafeon, and Sylveon.

Biology

Glaceon is a quadrupedal, mammalian Pokémon covered in light-blue fur. It has long, pointed ears, dark eyes, and a small nose. A teal crest with two dangling flaps adorns its head, resembling hair or headgear. Glaceon has two dark blue, rhombus-shaped markings on its back; the tip of its tail, feet, and flaps are the same shade of blue.

By quickly lowering its body temperature to −75 °F (−60 °C), Glaceon can freeze its fur into needle-sharp icicles that it can fire. This is used for a number of purposes, such as self-defense, tackling prey, or staying safe while it sleeps.[1] Doing so also lets it instantly freeze the moisture in the atmosphere with just a single cry to create small ice crystals and a flurry of diamond dust or ice pellets at its food. The diamond dust is noted to be gem-like in nature and glitters as it flutters around. Those captivated by Glaceon's beautiful snowfall will be frozen without realizing it. It is popular in ski resorts for its ability to create powdery snow from the coldness it generates.

Evolution data

Glaceon evolves from Eevee when exposed to an Ice Rock or, from Generation VIII onwards, when exposed to an Ice Stone. Eevee will always evolve into Glaceon when leveled up near an Ice Rock, even if the evolutionary conditions for it to evolve into Espeon, Umbreon, or Sylveon have also been met.

The Ice Rock is not present in Pokémon HeartGold and SoulSilver, so Eevee cannot evolve into Glaceon in these games, as well as Pokémon Sword and Shield and Pokémon Scarlet and Violet, although Eevee can evolve into Glaceon with the Ice Stone in these games instead. The Ice Stone is not obtainable in Pokémon Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl, so Eevee can only evolve into Glaceon with the Ice Rock. Eevee will never evolve into Glaceon in Pokémon: Let's Go, Pikachu! and Let's Go, Eevee!, as Glaceon is not present in those games.

Trivia

  • Glaceon was designed by Atsuko Nishida.[2][3]
  • Glaceon shares its category with Vanillite. They are both known as the Fresh Snow Pokémon.
  • Glaceon's Japanese name is the same as the English name of Glacia, a member of the Hoenn Elite Four.
  • According to the Diamond and Pearl Pokédex, Glaceon, Leafeon, and Umbreon have identical footprints.
  • Despite Eevee being obtainable before the Pokémon League in Pokémon Black 2 and White 2, it cannot evolve into Glaceon or Leafeon until after the Elite Four is defeated. This is because Twist Mountain, where the Ice Rock is located, cannot be reached until that point in the game, as the entrances to it and the Tubeline Bridge are blocked.
  • In Generation IX, prior to the Version 2.0.1 update of Pokémon Scarlet and Violet, Glaceon was unable to learn Trailblaze via TM, while its pre-Evolution and all the other Eeveelutions could.
  • According to the Pokémon Diamond and Pearl beta and the data files of Pokémon Battle Revolution,[4] Glaceon's developmental name was Aisia. This name may be derived from ice.

Origin

Like many of the Eeveelutions, Glaceon shares characteristics with foxes, dogs, and cats. Its habitat being cold areas suggests that it may be more closely based on an arctic fox. Assuming Leafeon draws inspiration from Siamese or other colorpoint cats, Glaceon may also draw some inspiration from them (especially blue points). Its "hair" resembles an ushanka or a scarf hat.

Name origin

Glaceon may be a combination of glacier (a large, slow-moving mass of ice on land) or glace (French for ice) and eon (an immeasurably long period of time, possibly referring to how long evolution takes naturally). Eon is a suffix all Eeveelutions share and was Eevee's English prototype name. Incidentally, its name is also an anagram of congeal (to become solid, usually by cooling).

Glacia may be derived from glacier. It may also involve grace.
